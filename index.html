The Greatest Show Choreography Guide (With Song Cues)

Made by Nathaniel Cruz
Lead Choreographer: Thomas Jimenez

Formation & Positions

  • Lead Position: Center front (Instructor). The lead projects confidence, initiates transitions, and cues the group.
  • Background Positions: Evenly spaced behind the lead, forming a staggered line for visibility. Each dancer mirrors the lead’s energy and timing, but may have unique accent moves for visual layering.

1. Introduction (Counts 1–8)

Song Cue: “Whoa-oh-oh-oh…” (opening vocalizations)
Counts Lead Background
1–2 Raise right arm overhead in a wide arc, palm open, gaze follows hand. Stand tall, feet hip-width apart. Mirror the lead’s right arm arc. Maintain posture and spacing.
3–4 Raise left arm to meet right, forming a symmetrical “welcome” pose. Hold for a beat. Mirror the lead, arms overhead, fingertips nearly touching. Hold for a beat.
5–6 Step right, swing both arms outward to shoulder height, palms out. Chest open, posture strong. Step right, arms outward. Slightly stagger the timing for a ripple effect if desired.
7–8 Step left, swing arms back in front, crossing wrists. Hold for a split second before next move. Step left, cross arms at wrists. Hold and prepare for transition.

2. Main Sequence (Counts 9–32)

A. Side Steps & Arm Swings (Counts 9–16)

Song Cue: “Ladies and gents, this is the moment you’ve waited for…”
Counts Lead Background
9–12 Step right, swing right arm across chest at shoulder height, palm down. Step left, swing left arm. Arms fully extended, sharp movements. Mirror the lead’s steps and arm swings. Focus on matching energy and timing.
13–16 Repeat side steps, add a bounce in knees for energy. Arms swing wider, push off floor for springiness. Repeat, but add a slight delay for a wave effect or match the lead exactly for unity.

B. Coordinated Turns (Counts 17–24)

Song Cue: “Been searching in the dark, your sweat soaking through the floor…”
Counts Lead Background
17–20 Step forward right, pivot 180° left on ball of foot, arms extended outward, fingers spread. Spot the audience as you turn. Follow the lead’s turn, arms synchronized. Maintain formation and spacing.
21–24 Step back to original spot, sweep arms overhead in a circular motion, end with arms open wide. Movements controlled and deliberate. Step back, arms overhead, match the lead’s timing. Hold open pose for a beat.

C. Full-Body Engagement (Counts 25–32)

Song Cue: “And buried in your bones there’s an ache that you can’t ignore…”
Counts Lead Background
25–28 Step forward right, punch right fist upward with force, twist torso. Repeat with left. Non-punching arm tight to side. Step forward, alternate punches, match lead’s energy. Slightly stagger for visual layering if desired.
29–32 Step back, cross arms in front of chest, then open wide, palms forward, chest lifted. Hold open position for a beat. Step back, cross and open arms. Hold and prepare for chorus.

3. Chorus Sequence (Counts 33–48)

A. Energetic Jumps (Counts 33–40)

Song Cue: “This is the greatest show!”
Counts Lead Background
33–40 Jump feet apart, arms overhead in strong “V”, fingers spread. Land softly, knees bent. Jump feet together, arms down. Repeat, projecting excitement and smiling. Jump with the lead, arms in “V”. Optionally, alternate arm angles or add a ripple for visual effect.

B. Freestyle Flair (Counts 41–48)

Song Cue: “We light it up, we won’t come down…”
Counts Lead Background
41–48 Add a spin or clap above head, big expressive movement. Use the music’s energy, make it theatrical. Freestyle in place—claps, spins, high knees, or dramatic poses. Express personality but stay in formation and complement the lead.

4. Finale (Counts 49–64)

A. Group Pose (Counts 49–56)

Song Cue: “And the sun can’t stop us now…”
Counts Lead Background
49–56 Step forward confidently, arms outstretched, fingers splayed, big smile. Hold for 4 counts, then step back. Project pride and accomplishment. Step forward, arms out, smile. Optionally, angle arms for a layered look. Hold, then step back in sync with lead.

B. Final Arm Sweep (Counts 57–64)

Song Cue: “This is the greatest show!” (final chorus)
Counts Lead Background
57–64 Sweep right arm across body, then left, ending with both arms overhead in a triumphant finish. Hold final pose, chest lifted, chin up, smile wide. Mirror the lead, arms overhead, unified celebratory pose. Freeze until music fades.

Tips for Practice

  • Watch the lead dancer for cues on timing and energy. The lead sets the pace for every transition.
  • Keep formations tight for visual impact. Use peripheral vision to maintain spacing.
  • Use big, expressive arm movements to match the theatrical style of the song. Don’t be afraid to exaggerate for the audience.
  • Smile and project confidence throughout the routine! Your facial expressions are as important as your movements.
  • Practice each section slowly, then gradually increase speed to match the music.
  • Record your practice and review to spot areas for improvement.
